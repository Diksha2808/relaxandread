Deep Breath

It sends a message to your brain to calm down and relax.

Breathing exercises can help you relax because they make your body feel like it does when you are already relaxed. By taking deep breaths your heart rate shows, more oxygen enters our bloodstream and ultimately communicates with the brain to relax. When you inhale, your heart rate speeds up. When you exhale, it slows down. Breathing in for a count of four and out for a count of eight for just a few minutes can start to calm your nervous system.

Friends and Family

Spend some time with your family

Friends & family are two important pillars of support, especially in difficult times. Research suggests maintaining strong friendships can help you cope with stress more effectively and help lower your chances of facing some types of stress in the first place. Similarly when you and your family spend quality time together, you can prevent stress related health issues and avoid unhealthy stress coping mechanisms such as binge eating or smoking.

  • Critical importance of friends and family

Be Happy

Happier people live longer.

While you may not be able to control all of the factors that are causing stress in your life, there are a few things you can do Just Chill ! Yes sometimes you just need to chill and if you regularly make time for fun and relaxation, you'll be in a better place to handle life's stressors. The act of laughing helps your body live happily. Also research has shown that being happier may help keep your immune system strong .

  • Secret of happiness

Music

Some research suggests that Music might even make you healthier

Music has the ability to make us dance like no one is watching, sing until your throat is sore and uncontrollably tap your feet.when you’re having a bad day, there’s nothing better than playing an uplifting and joyous song as loud as you can. Music’s ability to be a stress reliever should not be downplayed. Whether playing in the background or you’re giving it your full attention, certain genres of music have the innate ability to reduce stress. Soft, ambient music provides calming stimulation for the mind. In this case, best avoid loud rock or metal to moderate your stress.

  • Music and its power

Sleep

Poor sleep itself can even turn into a source of stress.

It’s no secret that sleep plays an important role in good physical and mental health. A good night's sleep makes you able to tackle the day's stress more easily. When you’re tired, you’re less patient and more easily agitated, which can increase stress. Most adults need 7 to 9 hours of sleep each night. Practicing good sleep hygiene along with stress-lowering tactics can help improve your quality of sleep.

  • Quality Sleep

Exploring Nature

Being in nature, or even viewing scenes of nature, reduces anger, fear, and stress

New research from an interdisciplinary Cornell team has found that as little as 10 minutes in a natural setting can help college students feel happier and lessen the effects of both physical and mental stress.Spending time in nature is linked to both cognitive benefits and improvements in mood, mental health and emotional well-being.Feeling connected to nature can produce similar benefits to well-being, regardless of how much time one spends outdoors.

Coffee

It sends a message to your brain to calm down and relax.

Studies have shown that coffee consumption can reduce depression and suicide risk, as well as relieve stress. Small to moderate amounts of caffeine can lift your mood and give you a boost. However, high amounts of caffeine can lead to the negative health effects associated with prolonged elevated levels of cortisol (as in chronic stress).
Some points to keep in mind

  • Don’t take too much Caffeine
  • Avoid caffeine after 2 p.m.
  • Enjoy caffeine with physical activity

Exercise

Spend some time on health improvement

Exercise increases your overall health and your sense of well-being however it also has some direct stress-busting benefits. Regular exercise can increase self-confidence, improve your mood, help you relax. Exercise can also improve your sleep, which is often disrupted by stress, depression and anxiety. Note If you haven't exercised for some time or you have health concerns, you may want to talk to your doctor before starting a new exercise routine.

  • Exercise and its benefits

Meditation

A simple, fast way to reduce stress

Meditation can reduce stress levels, which translates to less anxiety. By learning to calm your body and mind, your physical and emotional stress can melt away. This leaves you feeling better, refreshed, and ready to face the challenges of your day with a healthy attitude. With regular practice over weeks or months, you can experience even greater benefits.

  • Power of Meditation

Gratitude

Focus on what you have

Expressing gratitude helps people feel more positive emotions, cherish good experiences, and think more deeply about what they have rather than what they lack. If someone in your life selflessly does something for you and you want to express gratitude, common expressions of thanks might not seem like enough. Gratitude helps people refocus on what they have instead of what they lack. And, although it may feel contrived at first, this mental state grows stronger with use and practice.

  • Thankyou!

Hobby

Reading||Arts and Crafts||Yoga||Baking

Hobbies aren’t just about fun and games, they range from discussing your latest book to baking the perfect brioche. Regardless of your hobby, it can have great benefits for your mind, body, social life and even your career. Hobbies such as gardening, hiking, dancing, yoga, sports or fitness can keep us active without the feeling of an obligatory trip to the gym. Hobbies can also help you boost your career.

Have A Laugh

Improve your mood

Laughter is well-known for its therapeutic effects, and many places now offer laughter therapy or laughter yoga classes to those seeking to boost their health and mood. Studies have in fact found that even the anticipation of laughter can help us to relax by lifting our spirits and reducing stress levels in the body. If you are serious about your relaxation, you could consider attending a laughter class, but chilling out with a funny DVD, book or friend is an equally great option to help you relax.
